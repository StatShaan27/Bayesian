1. Introduction to Bayesian Inference

This section introduces the concept of Bayesian inference, contrasting it with classical approaches. Key points include the formulation of prior beliefs and updating them with data to form posterior distributions.

2. Prior, Likelihood, and Posterior

The Bayesian framework consists of three fundamental components:

  • Prior: Represents prior knowledge or beliefs about a parameter.
  • Likelihood: The probability of observed data given the parameter.
  • Posterior: Updated beliefs combining prior and likelihood via Bayes’ theorem.

3. Decision Theory Basics

Decision theory provides a framework for making choices under uncertainty. Concepts of loss functions and risk are introduced, forming the foundation for Bayesian decision-making.

4. Examples and Applications

Illustrative examples of Bayesian updating and simple decision problems are presented. Future sections will expand these with R code snippets and numerical simulations.
